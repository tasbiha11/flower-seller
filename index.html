About Us

After a job at another plant nursery, Robyn Walters was planning to sell a few flowers at a stand on Fourth Street and move out of town. That's when landscaper Art Escobedo, along with his dog, Shaniqua, proposed they make a go of the plant business 10 years ago. Walters saw an opportunity in a small outdoor section of a Price Chopper's on Fourth Street, and asked if she could sell flowers there before the grocery store closed. And so was born Viola's Flower Garden, the open-air East Route 66 plant nursery that sells shrubs, flowers, annuals, perennials, fertilizers, soil-improvers and trees. "It was not a planned business at all," Walters said, but it started a month after the idea was hatched. Viola's has built repeat customers and employees who stay for years. Growing from the former Kmart parking lot, Viola's moved to East Route 66 in 2006. The place is named for Walters' gardening grandmother, who was no longer alive when it opened, and was launched with the donated labor of friends and family.Customers go there primarily for the flowers, because they can get in and out easily, for the staff of gardeners who help select plants, and because they know Shaniqua, Walters and Escobedo said.
